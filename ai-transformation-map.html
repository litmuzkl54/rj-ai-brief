🎯 Operating Model (Organization & Culture)

Why This Is the Primary Blocker

You cannot hold engineers accountable for business outcomes if the model doesn't define what "accountability" or "autonomy" means in practice. This block must be solved first because it establishes the foundation for everything else.

What Must Change

Clarify interfaces—who decides, who owns, and how governance is embedded into delivery. Create mechanical, predictable decision-making processes.

Key Leverage Point

Weekly Governance & Decision Authority Matrix

A clear RACI embedded into every engagement ensures the operating model is more than a document—it's mechanical and flawless in practice.

Target State (12–18 Months)

  • ✓ Weekly governance is mechanical and flawless
  • ✓ All decisions made in under 48 hours
  • ✓ Scope discipline strictly enforced via mechanical change process
  • ✓ FDSE unit feels like core business, not experimental pilot

Dependencies

Unlocks
→ Skills & Competencies (can't define FDE role without this)
→ AI Solutions Portfolio (can't set governance for delivery without this)
📅 Timeline & Milestones
Months 1–3: Design Phase Map current decision flows, identify bottlenecks, draft RACI matrix
Months 4–9: Pilot & Embed Test governance model on first 2–3 engagements, iterate, make mechanical
Months 10–18: Scale & Institutionalize Roll out across all FDSE engagements, measure compliance and velocity

⚡ Skills & Competencies

Why It Depends on Operating Model

Once the FDE (Forward-Deployed Engineer) role and its decision rights are defined in the operating model, LTM can address the "change management nightmare" of reskilling senior practitioners resistant to moving from specialist roles (BA, Dev, Architect) to autonomous generalists.

The Core Challenge

Senior practitioners are experts in their specialties. Asking them to become AI-powered generalists is a hard cultural shift. The operating model must first prove that this new role has clear autonomy, decision rights, and career value.

Key Leverage Point

FDE Role Definition & Compensation Model

Make FDSE a desirable career path with 20% higher base pay and 30–50% outcome-based bonuses. Collapse talent resistance and incentivize efficiency.

Target State (12–18 Months)

  • ✓ Stable cohort of 8–12 trained FDEs
  • ✓ Standardized 4-week ramp time
  • ✓ <5% attrition rate
  • ✓ >80% adoption of AI acceleration tools (Claude, Cursor, etc.)

The FDE Training Arc

  • Week 1: Role clarity, autonomy boundaries, decision authority
  • Week 2: AI tool mastery (Claude, Cursor, GitHub Copilot)
  • Week 3: Customer engagement patterns, scope discipline
  • Week 4: Live deal support with mentor, handoff readiness

Dependencies

Depends On
← Operating Model (must define FDE role & autonomy first)
Unlocks
→ AI Solutions Portfolio (now you have people to deliver it)
📅 Timeline & Milestones
Months 1–2: Design Comp Model Design FDE role, comp structure, career path, announce to team
Months 3–5: Recruit & Onboard Cohort 1 Select 3–4 volunteers, run first 4-week training, place on deal
Months 6–18: Scale to Full Cohort Onboard 8–12 FDEs total, measure attrition & tool adoption, refine model

🎁 AI Solutions Portfolio

Why It Ties Everything Together

The portfolio must shift from conceptual frameworks to concrete operational assets. This ensures that the efficiencies gained by FDEs are captured as margin rather than lost in the T&M "hours" trap.

The Core Problem

Without standardized portfolio offers, FDEs optimize for utilization (billing hours), not margin. The portfolio operationalizes AI-powered delivery into repeatable, pre-priced offerings that turn speed into profit.

Key Leverage Point

AI Usage Standardization & Measurement

Define exactly how AI tools are used in each role and document prompts that work. Make AI a predictable part of LTM's proprietary methodology and IP, not a random tool.

Target State (12–18 Months)

  • ✓ 85%+ of new FDSE deals start from approved, pre-priced portfolio offers
  • ✓ Example: Lead Qualification at $50K, Design Sprint at $75K, etc.
  • ✓ 40%+ average margins on portfolio deals vs. 28% for custom T&M
  • ✓ Portfolio becomes LTM's competitive differentiator

Portfolio Examples (Operationalized)

  • Lead Qualification Sprint: $50K, 2 weeks, AI-powered customer research & positioning
  • Design Sprint + AI Prototyping: $75K, 3 weeks, Claude-accelerated design & technical validation
  • AI-Powered Roadmap Audit: $40K, 10 days, competitor analysis + roadmap recommendations
  • Rapid Engineering Baseline: $60K, 2 weeks, architecture blueprint + codebase assessment via AI

Dependencies

Depends On
← Operating Model (governance for delivery & pricing)
← Skills & Competencies (people trained to deliver it)
📅 Timeline & Milestones
Months 1–4: Standardize & Document Map AI tool usage across FDE roles, document repeatable prompts, create delivery playbooks
Months 5–9: Price & Validate Set margins on 3–4 core offers, test pricing on pilot deals, measure margin reality
Months 10–18: Scale & Iterate Launch full portfolio, track close rate & margins, add new offers based on FDE feedback
